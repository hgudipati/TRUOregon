What is TRU?

A transect from
rural to urban

TRU Oregon is a research and community platform co-led by Southern Oregon University (SOU) and Portland State University (PSU). The transect runs 280 miles — from Ashland's Siskiyou foothills to Portland's Willamette riverfront — tracing Oregon's climate future in real time.

Public-funded universities provide education to communities. TRU Oregon is at the front of this.

Ashland sits at the southern end of the TRU transect and at the frontier of Oregon's climate crisis: longer fire seasons, declining snowpack, drought-stressed watersheds, and compounding emergencies that are already reshaping daily life in the Rogue Valley.

Portland is tracking toward similar conditions within two decades. By connecting SOU's rural knowledge with PSU's urban research, the TRU corridor compresses learning timelines and builds more equitable, community-grounded responses to the challenges ahead.

TRU Oregon is built on community knowledge. If you're working on something related to water, fire, climate adaptation, or resilience anywhere along the Ashland–Portland transect, we want to feature it.

  • 01 Fill out our community project form with your project details and location.
  • 02 Our SOU or PSU team reviews submissions and reaches out to learn more.
  • 03 Your project gets featured on TRU Oregon and connected to similar work across the corridor.
  • 04 All data lives in a shared Google Sheet — open, accessible, community-owned.
